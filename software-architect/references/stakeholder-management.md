# Stakeholder Management for Software Architects

## Core Responsibility
The architect acts as a central point of contact and a bridge between business education CEOs/managers and technical development teams. They must reconcile customer requirements with technical feasibility.

## Key Stakeholder Groups
- **Management/Product Owners:** Concerned with cost, time-to-market, and business value.
- **Developers:** Concerned with technical constraints, interfaces, and implementation clarity.
- **QA/Testers:** Concerned with system testability and quality requirements.
- **Operations/Admins:** Concerned with infrastructure, deployment, and system reliability.

## Critical Tasks
- **Identify Concerns:** Use interviews or the Architecture Communication Canvas to uncover implicit concerns that may not be in official requirements.
- **Manage the "Magic Rectangle":** Balance the competing goals of **cost, time, functionality, and quality**.
- **Conflict Resolution:** Identify and mediate conflicts between short-term business wins and long-term system maintainability.
- **Iterative Feedback:** Systematically seek feedback from stakeholders during the design process to handle uncertainty.

## Communication Strategies
- **Tailored Detail:** Explain the architecture to customers via business benefits and to developers via technical specifications.
- **Harmonisation:** Ensure that verbal explanations match written documentation to avoid ambiguity.
- **Visualization:** Use tools like "Architecture Wallpapers" or overview presentations to facilitate interactive discussion.
